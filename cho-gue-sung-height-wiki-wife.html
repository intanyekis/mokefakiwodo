Cho Gue Sung Height, Early Life & Career

Early Life: - Cho Gue Sung was born on 25 January 1998 in Ansan, Gyeonggi, South Korea. Cho Gue Sung spent his youth career and freshman year at Gwangju University as a defensive midfielder. However, Cho Gue Sung was not regarded as a decent midfielder by his university manager, Lee Seung-Won, and thus switched to a striker in 2017. He most goals in his professional debut season. He was done, and he moved to K League 1 team Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors the following year.

Career: - Cho Gue Sung made his senior debut for South Korea on September 7, 2021, in a World Cup qualifier against Lebanon. In November 2022, Cho Gue Sung was appointed to the 26-man roster for the 2022 FIFA World Cup. In Korea's second World Cup match, he scored two goals in a 3–2 loss against Ghana, becoming the first South Korean player to score two or more goals in a single World Cup match.
