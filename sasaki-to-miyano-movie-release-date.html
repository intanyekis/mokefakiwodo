Sasaki To Miyano Movie Release Date: Is This Movie Released?

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, June 17, 2024

The Sasaki and Miyano manga by Sho Harusono, which started publishing through pixiv Comic in 2016, has nine volumes so far, however, the series is still ongoing. It gained popularity rapidly, inspiring Kotoko Hachijo to create an adaption that is a two-volume novel.

In 2019, Harusono started creating a spin-off series called Hirano and Kagiura, which has three volumes so far. She did this because she realized how much people loved her characters. Sasaki and Miyano’s anime adaptation, which included 12 episodes and an OVA, was released in 2022.

North American audiences can watch the Studio Deen-produced show on Crunchyroll. The Sasaki and Miyano official Twitter account announced a continuation of the series as an animated film on June 10, 2022. A bonus short film centered on the Hirano and Kagura series will be released in addition to the main movie.

Sasaki To Miyano Movie Release Date

The upcoming film Sasaki and Miyano: Graduation has unveiled its main image, along with its release date, which has been scheduled for February 17, 2023. The movie’s adaptation of the “Graduation Chapter,” Sotsugyo-hen, will show how the two main characters handle Sasaki’s graduation. Together with the movie, a short film based on the Hirano and Kagiura manga by Sh Harusono will be shown.

Sasaki To Miyano 

The developing romance between two adolescent boys is the subject of Sasaki and Miyano. Yoshikazu Miyano helps Shuumei Sasaki break up a quarrel among classmates, and Shuumei Sasaki immediately falls head over heels for him.

Unaware that Miyano only appears to read Boys’ Love (BL), a genre that concentrates on male romantic relationships, Sasaki asks if he can borrow any manga in an effort to get to know Miyano better. Sasaki and Miyano quickly become inseparable as they talk about the manga they’ve been reading.

Following Sasaki’s brave confession of love, Miyano finds it difficult to comprehend his feelings and requires some time to come to a conclusion regarding their relationship. Harusono’s storytelling acknowledges the various concerns of BL stories, such as how they could affect a young person’s perception of their own identity, while still praising the genre as a whole.

Fans appreciate how Miyano defies numerous BL preconceptions in the series, which has received praise for its treatment of adolescent love and se*uality (even as he attempts to navigate his experiences using those very tropes).

He uses the knowledge he gains through reading to strengthen his bonds with others, gaining confidence and better communication skills in the process. He demonstrates how BL can serve as both a fun read for readers and an effective tool for normalizing same-s*x relationships.
